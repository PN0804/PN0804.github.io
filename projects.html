Max MSP

Press It

(2025) by Phi Nguyen

Creating Press It allowed me to explore interactive media through sound, timing, and user engagement with Max MSP. My goal was to reinterpret the game of Bop It while designing a version that reflected my own style of play and minimalism. Developing the game challenged me to think about how sound cues, visual prompts, and timing systems worked together to create something intuitive for the player to experience. Through out the process, I experimented with patch organization, logical structures, and real time input handling. Troubleshooting timing delays and refining audio feedback had taught me how small adjustments can dramatically affect the feel of an integrative piece. Building the project also pushed me to improve my workflow and create cleaner, more modular patches. In the end, Press it became more than a simple game to me, it became a study in user interaction, feedback design, and the balance between adversity and accessibility. Overall, the project strengthened my confidence in Max MSP and opened up new possibilities for future interactive or game based work.

Overgrown Future

(2025) by Phi Nguyen

In this piece, I imagined a future that has been reclaimed by nature, with a city left abandoned and ravaged by plant life. The pink sky represents the new atmosphere after humanity's decline, while the overgrown towers and other scattered remnants illustrate the remains of humanity. The figure in the foreground is a remnant of humanity that explores the world and whatever was left. This was inspired by The Last of Us series where I tried to create a post apocalyptic scifi world with aspects of ecological rebirth. I used personal photos of plants and the sky along with found images of buildings, an abandoned sign, and an abandoned car. Through layering, masking, and color blending, I created a scene that feels a bit haunting yet also hopeful where nature is able to heal the Earth's scars. Overall the decaying structures in combination with the vibrant growth of nature demonstrates how life can continue to adapt despite having undergone desolation.

Waking Up

(2025) by Phi Nguyen

My sound walk, Waking Up, captures the everyday rhythm of preparing for a new day. I chose this theme because it is a universal experience, the quiet progression from resting to moving, and from stillness to focus. The walk follows multiple stops that could mirror a typical morning of a student from waking up to an alarm, moving through the house, brushing teeth, making breakfast, grabbing essentials, and finally driving off to begin your day. I recorded natural household sounds like footsteps, door creaks, running water, and rustling bags. I then layered them with some reverb, fading, and some other effects to create some depth and realism. Through this piece, I tried to reflect how ordinary sounds can shape our routine, taking something as simple as a mourning routine into an immersive sensory experience.

Wifi Down

(2025) by Phi Nguyen

My parody trailer is loosely based on the Joker final trailer. I chose this trailer for its psychological arc, emotional intensity, and the dramatic pacing that aligns well with the emotional rollercoaster I wanted to portray in my story. The original trailer follows a character that becomes frustrated into breaking down and undergoes a transformation, that my protagonist similarly undergoes. Due to the movie's dark, grounded psychological drama, creating a parody that was one to one was challenging to come up with. Many of the scenes, environments, and performances are not easily or ethically replicated so I aimed to emulate the trait as closely as possible by focusing on its tone, pacing, color shifts, and dramatic escalation. My parody adapts the key moments of the original trailer into a relatable wifi meltdown moment. Act 1 focuses on illustrating intense gameplay in an online competitive environment, Act 2 follows descent into depression and hallucination as the router refuses to reconnect, and Act 3 is a resolution for the protagonist to find a solution and regain hope. Through lighting choices, close ups, transitions, and mood based color grading, I recreated the spirit and emotion of the Joker trailer while reimagining it into a comedic wifi crisis.
